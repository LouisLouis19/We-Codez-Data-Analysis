2. Athletes Dataset

Identify and list out the issues present in the data, then list out the steps you would take to clean this dataset. Utilize the guiding questions below to provide your analysis of the cleaned data, feel free to clean, analyze, and explore the dataset beyond these guiding questions.

Guiding Questions

1. Which countries produce the most successful athletes across different disciplines?

How do we clean and process the data to determine which countries have the highest number of athletes excelling in various disciplines, factoring in missing or inconsistent country data and discipline classifications?

2. How does the success rate of athletes vary with age?

What trends can be identified when analyzing how age correlates with success across different sports disciplines, considering outliers, missing birthdates, and the need to standardize the calculation of age from inconsistent date formats?

3. How do physical attributes like height and weight correlate with success in different sports disciplines?

How can we clean the height and weight columns, handle outliers, and then analyze how these physical attributes contribute to performance in different sports?

4. How do athletes' birthplaces and residence locations influence their success?

What patterns can be identified between where athletes are born versus where they reside and their success in different sports, considering missing and inconsistent location data?

5. How does the presence of sporting relatives impact an athlete’s performance and career longevity?

How do we clean and process the sporting_relatives column, considering multiple entries and missing data, and analyze the effect of having relatives in sports on an athlete’s success?

6. Is there a significant correlation between the number of years an athlete has been in their sport and their success?

How can we clean and process data around athletes' start dates or career lengths (if available) and link this to their performance, adjusting for missing or inconsistent information?

7. How do factors like education level and occupation influence athletes' performance across disciplines?

What trends can be drawn when comparing the education levels or occupations of athletes with their success in their sports, considering missing or incomplete data in these fields?

3. Data Science Jobs Dataset

Identify and list out the issues present in the data, then list out the steps you would take to clean this dataset. Utilize the guiding questions below to provide your analysis of the cleaned data, feel free to clean, analyze, and explore the dataset beyond these guiding questions.

Guiding Questions

1. How do salary estimates compare across different company sizes, adjusting for outliers and inconsistent salary formats?

This requires cleaning the Salary Estimate column to extract numeric values, dealing with ranges, adjusting for outliers, and mapping the salary to company sizes across inconsistent formats.

2. What trends can be observed between company ratings and the job titles offered by companies, accounting for inconsistencies in job title naming conventions?

This requires grouping and standardizing job titles that may have slight variations, then correlating them with company ratings while addressing missing or misleading ratings.

3. How does job location (city/state) impact salary, considering both location data inconsistencies and salary formatting issues?

Cleaning Location and Salary Estimate columns will be necessary, standardizing city and state data, and resolving salary discrepancies, including ranges and estimated values.

4. How do factors like industry and sector correlate with company success, using revenue as a proxy, considering missing and vague revenue data?

Requires cleaning the Revenue column, standardizing formats (e.g., ranges, missing values, and ambiguous entries like "Unknown"), and performing multi-variable analysis across Industry and Sector.

5. How does the year a company was founded correlate with its rating, industry, and revenue, after handling missing or erroneous founding years and dealing with categorical data issues?

This involves cleaning the Founded column to address missing, incorrect, or nonsensical founding years and analyzing how older versus newer companies perform across multiple dimensions.

6. How do salary estimates differ for the same job title between companies of different ownership types, requiring extensive data cleaning across both salary and company ownership data?

Cleaning Type of ownership and Salary Estimate is necessary to ensure consistent categories and formats, followed by cross-analysis between these variables.

7. What influence do competitors listed in the dataset have on the success of a company, in terms of salary and rating, considering missing and incomplete competitor data?

Cleaning the Competitors column (e.g., missing data, entries like -1, multiple competitors in one field) will be essential before correlating this information with salary, ratings, and company performance.

4. News Sentiment Analysis Dataset

Identify and list out the issues present in the data, then list out the steps you would take to clean this dataset. Utilize the guiding questions below to provide your analysis of the cleaned data, feel free to clean, analyze, and explore the dataset beyond these guiding questions.

Guiding Questions

1. How do sentiment trends differ across news sources over time, accounting for inconsistencies in date formatting and missing sentiment data?

This requires cleaning the Published At column to ensure consistent date formats, handling missing sentiment values, and performing temporal analysis across different sources.

2. What are the most frequent topics associated with each sentiment category, considering noisy and inconsistent text in titles and descriptions?

Cleaning the Title and Description fields to remove irrelevant information and standardize terms is needed before performing a topic modeling analysis to identify common topics per sentiment category.

3. How do sentiment distributions vary by news type (e.g., Business, Sports, Politics), after addressing missing or ambiguous news type entries?

Cleaning the Type column, ensuring consistent categorization of news types, and analyzing sentiment distributions across these categories will be required.

4. How does the author’s identity correlate with sentiment trends across articles, considering missing or ambiguous author data?

Cleaning the Author column, resolving missing entries, and standardizing names are needed to explore the correlation between specific authors and the sentiment they tend to express in their articles.

5. How does the time of publication influence the sentiment of news articles, considering missing or incorrect timestamps in the published dates?

Cleaning and standardizing the Published At column to extract accurate time information, dealing with missing data, and analyzing how publication time (e.g., morning vs. evening) affects sentiment trends.

6. Which sources are the most polarizing (i.e., have the most extreme sentiment distributions), considering missing data and duplicate entries?

Cleaning the Source column, removing duplicates, handling missing data, and performing a sentiment analysis to determine which sources tend to have the most extreme positive or negative sentiments.
